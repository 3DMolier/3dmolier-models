Typical project requests

Common requests include “make a similar model with different equipment,” “optimize this asset for real-time,” “create a missing vehicle from references,” “prepare a cutaway view,” “add rigging to moving parts,” or “build a branded product model for marketing animation.”

What affects the price

  • Model complexity and number of visible parts.
  • Quality and completeness of references.
  • Texture realism and material requirements.
  • Rigging, animation, moving parts and technical accuracy.
  • Required formats, deadline and revision scope.

Required references

Send front, side, rear and top views when possible. Add dimensions, screenshots, CAD references, material notes, color references, required scale and examples of the final visual style.

Supported formats

Typical targets include 3ds Max, Blender, Maya, Cinema 4D, FBX, OBJ, STL, Unreal Engine, Unity and common rendering workflows. Confirm required formats before production starts.

Mini FAQ

What should I send for a quote?

Send reference photos, dimensions, target formats, deadline, level of detail, intended software and whether the model needs animation or rigging.

What affects the price?

Complexity, amount of reference, required accuracy, textures, rigging, formats, deadline and number of final deliverables affect price.

Can you make a model similar to an existing TurboSquid asset?

Yes. Send the product link and explain what needs to change.

Can I request models for Unreal Engine or Unity?

Yes. Include target engine, polygon budget, texture limits and required export formats.
